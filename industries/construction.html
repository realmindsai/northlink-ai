The numbers behind construction in Melbourne's north

With 20,807 construction businesses, Melbourne's north is one of the fastest-growing building corridors in Australia. From residential builders and electricians to civil contractors and plumbers, the region is experiencing sustained demand driven by population growth and infrastructure investment. Despite this growth, most businesses still rely on manual takeoffs, handwritten site diaries, and ad-hoc variation tracking.

Where AI fits in a typical construction workflow

AI fits into the construction workflow at three labour-intensive stages: quantity takeoffs (reading floor plans and generating material lists), progress reporting (analysing site photos and generating formatted reports), and variation management (assembling correspondence, diary entries, and cost records into structured claims). Each of these tasks currently consumes hours of skilled time that could be spent on-site.

What other construction businesses are doing with AI right now

Builders and trades in Melbourne's north are starting to use AI to automate quantity takeoffs from architectural plans, generate progress reports from site photos, and compile variation claims from scattered communications. These aren't replacing tradies — they're eliminating the paperwork that keeps them in the office instead of on the tools.

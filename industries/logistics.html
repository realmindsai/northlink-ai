The numbers behind logistics in Melbourne's north

Melbourne's north is a logistics powerhouse, with 17,700 transport and logistics businesses operating across freight corridors connecting the port, airport, and interstate routes. From last-mile delivery fleets to long-haul carriers, these businesses move billions of dollars worth of goods annually. Paper-based proof of delivery, manual route planning, and invoice disputes remain persistent pain points across the industry.

Where AI fits in a typical logistics workflow

AI integrates into logistics operations at three high-friction points: POD reconciliation (reading crumpled delivery dockets and matching them to invoices), route optimisation (dynamically redistributing deliveries when drivers or vehicles are unavailable), and invoice reconciliation (comparing carrier charges against contracted rates to catch overcharges automatically).

What other logistics businesses are doing with AI right now

Transport operators in Melbourne's north are deploying AI to digitise proof-of-delivery workflows, automatically rebalance routes when disruptions occur, and audit carrier invoices against contracts in seconds rather than hours. These tools pay for themselves within weeks by recovering overcharges and eliminating manual data entry.
